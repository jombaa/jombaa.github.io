This was a training simulation.

No information was collected or transmitted. Here are the red flags this scenario demonstrates:

  • Urgency and scarcity: “Only today!” pressure tactic.
  • Unexpected reward from a famous brand you didn’t request.
  • Request for your account password to claim a prize—legitimate offers never ask for that.
  • Suspicious domain or hosting that doesn’t match the brand.

What to do in real life:

  • Don’t enter passwords on promo pages; go directly to the official site by typing the URL.
  • Verify sender address and links before clicking. Hover to inspect URLs.
  • Report suspected phish to your IT/security team.
